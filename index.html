Distance and coverage by lens

Effective distance depends on lens (mm) and sensor. Approximate data for face/license plate recognition:

2.8 mm

Very wide angle (90°-100°). Range: up to 8-12 m (detection).

3.6 mm

Medium angle (70°-80°). Range: 15-20 m recognition.

4 mm

Standard (60°-70°). Range: 20-25 m identification.

6 mm

Telephoto (40°-50°). Range: 30-40 m.

8 mm

Long range (30°-35°). Up to 50-60 m.

12 mm +

Optical zoom. More than 80 m (ideal for PTZ).

Night vision IR distance is usually shorter (e.g., 30m on bullet IR). Always check specifications.

Installation steps (professional guide)

  1. Planning and design: Define blind spots, angles, camera type per area. Make a sketch.
  2. Choose location and height: Minimum 2.5-3 m to avoid vandalism, but enough to capture faces.
  3. Cabling (if applicable): For analog/PoE use UTP or coaxial cable. Protect with conduits. For wireless, ensure WiFi signal.
  4. Physical mounting: Fix the base with screws and anchors, route cables, secure the camera.
  5. Connection to DVR/NVR or PoE switch: Connect each camera to the recorder or PoE injector. If IP, configure network.
  6. Initial configuration: Access menu (local or app). Set IP, date/time, recording quality, motion detection.
  7. Image adjustment and focus: From the app or auxiliary monitor, focus and adjust zoom/angle.
  8. Remote viewing test: Verify access from mobile app, notifications, and playback.
  9. Storage: Configure hard drive (overwrite), cloud, or SD card. Estimate backup days.

Additional key information

Storage

DVR (analog) / NVR (IP). Special hard drives for surveillance (Western Purple, SkyHawk). Calculation: one 1080p camera uses ~8-12 GB/day.

PoE (Power over Ethernet)

Single UTP cable carries data and power (up to 100m). Ideal for IP cameras. Use PoE switch or injectors.

Night vision

Infrared (IR) or white light. IR LED range varies (10-60m). Avoid reflections on walls.

Cloud vs Local

Cloud: subscription, accessible anywhere. Local: more privacy, no recurring costs.

Network security

Change default passwords, use 2FA, separate WiFi network for cameras.

Dual lens / 180°

Fisheye or multi-sensor cameras cover wide areas without blind spots.
